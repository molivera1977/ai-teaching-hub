📸 Show AI Exactly What You Want

Use screenshots, snips, and photos to show AI a specific format, question style, or reading passage — so it copies your exact vision.

Step 1 of 7 — The Secret Superpower

🚀 Before you begin: Open one of these in a new tab — all are free to start. You'll use it to practice taking screenshots and uploading them as you go.
Claude or ChatGPT or Gemini or Copilot

🤯 The Secret Superpower: Showing Instead of Telling

You already know how to describe what you want in a prompt. But there's an even faster, more accurate method: show the AI a picture of it.

Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot, and Gemini can all read and understand images — including screenshots, photos of worksheets, and even pictures of handwritten notes.

📸 What is a screenshot?

A screenshot is a picture of whatever is currently showing on your computer screen — a Word document, a website, a PDF, a quiz, anything. Your computer takes the picture instantly and saves it as an image file you can share.

Think of it like holding up a piece of paper in front of someone so they can read it — except you're showing your screen to AI instead.

✂️ What is a snip?

A snip (short for "screen snip") is a cropped screenshot — instead of capturing your entire screen, you draw a box around just the part you want. It's faster and cleaner than a full screenshot because AI only sees exactly what you chose to show it.

🖥️ Full Screenshot — your whole screen
Everything on your screen captured at once — the browser, address bar, tabs, and all. Usually more than AI needs to see.
✂️ Snip big — the entire document
You drew a box around just the worksheet. AI sees the full document — title, layout, columns, images — nothing extra.
✂️ Snip small — just a section
Same document — but you only drew a box around the first few paragraphs. Use this when AI only needs part of the page.
✂️ Snip tiny — just one question
From a math test — a snip of just one question. AI reads the exact format, layout, and answer choices. Use this when you want AI to copy a specific style.
✂️ Snip — a two-part question
A reading question with multiple choice and a written response section. AI sees the exact format and can write more questions just like it.
✂️ Snip — an open-ended question
A plain text question with no answer choices. AI can write 10 more just like it — same wording style, same difficulty, same subject.
💡 You don't need to save anything. When you take a snip, it goes straight to your clipboard — the same invisible clipboard your computer uses for copy and paste. You paste it directly into AI, just like you'd paste text. No files to manage, no folders to open.

Step 2 of 7 — What AI Can See

👁️ What Can AI Actually See in a Screenshot?

When you upload or paste an image into Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or Copilot, the AI reads every detail — layout, text, formatting, structure, and style. Here's what it can pick up on:

📋
Question Format
Multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank, matching, short answer, and more
📖
Reading Level
Vocabulary difficulty and grade-level language of a passage
🖼️
Design & Layout
Column structure, font size, spacing, and overall page design
🔢
Answer Choices
How many options and how they're labeled — A/B/C/D or 1/2/3/4
✏️
Text Styling
Bold keywords, underlining, italics, and specific phrasing styles
📊
Tables & Rubrics
Rubric structures, table formats, and graphic organizer layouts
✅ Think of it this way: A screenshot is worth a thousand words of prompting. If you have an example of what you want, showing it is always faster and more accurate than describing it.

Step 3 of 7 — Snip & Paste

✂️ Snip & Paste — The Quick Way

This is the fastest method. You take a snip, it copies to your clipboard automatically — no file is saved to your computer. Then you switch to AI and paste it in. Start to finish in under 30 seconds. Select your computer type below:

Windows has a built-in snipping tool you can open instantly with a keyboard shortcut — no need to search for an app or go through any menus. This shortcut opens it immediately so you can draw a box around exactly what you want to capture.

The Windows key (⊞) is in the bottom-left corner of your keyboard — it has the Windows logo on it. Hold all three keys down at the same time (like pressing piano keys together), then release them.

This shortcut is the quickest way to open Windows' built-in snipping tool without ever leaving your screen. Think of it like picking up a pair of scissors — you're not cutting anything yet, you're just getting the tool ready.

Your screen will go slightly dim and your cursor will turn into a crosshair (+). That means it's open and waiting for you to draw your box.

2
Your screen goes slightly dim — that means it's ready!

Everything on your screen will get darker for a moment. Your mouse cursor will turn into a small plus sign (+). This is completely normal — it means the screenshot tool is active and waiting for you.

3
Left-click and drag to draw a box around exactly what you want to capture.

Left-click where you want your selection to start (top-left corner of what you want), hold the mouse button down, drag to the opposite corner (bottom-right), then release — a white box shows exactly what will be captured.

4
Let go of the mouse — it's automatically copied!

The moment you release the mouse button, the selected area is automatically copied to your clipboard (your clipboard is the invisible holding area where copied things wait — like when you press Ctrl+C on text). You'll see a small notification pop up in the bottom-right corner of your screen. You don't need to save anything — it's ready to paste.

✅ No file, no saving, no folder needed. Just go straight to Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or Copilot and paste it in.
5
Open Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or Copilot, left-click in the chat box, then right-click and choose Paste.

Your screenshot will appear right in the chat window. Then type your prompt — what you want AI to do with it — and press Enter. That's it! (You can also press Ctrl+V on Windows or Cmd+V on Mac if you prefer.)

You'll see your regular mouse pointer change into a small plus sign (+). The screen stays normal — nothing dims on a Mac. That plus sign means the screenshot tool is active and waiting for you to draw a selection.

Left-click where you want your selection to start (top-left corner of what you want), hold the mouse button down, drag to the opposite corner (bottom-right), then release — a blue box shows exactly what will be captured. Mac even shows you the pixel size as you drag!

4
Let go — you'll hear a camera left-click and it's on your clipboard!

The moment you release the mouse button, you'll hear a camera shutter sound (if your volume is on) and a thumbnail preview will appear in the bottom-right corner of your screen for a few seconds. The snip is automatically copied to your clipboard — you can paste it into AI right away. ✅ Pro tip — snip just what you need: You don't need to capture the whole screen. Draw your box tightly around just the one question, section, or format you want AI to copy. A small, focused snip gives AI cleaner information and better results.

Step 4 of 7 — Snip & Save

💾 Snip & Save — When You Want to Keep the File

📌 Paste vs. Save — which should you use?
  • Just paste it — when you need it once, right now. Quick question, quick answer. No file needed.
  • Save the file — when you want to use the same image more than once, share it with a colleague, or upload it to AI on a different day.

This step shows you how to find your saved snip and upload it to AI using the paperclip button. Select your computer type:

⚠️ On Windows, Win+Shift+S does NOT save a file automatically — it only copies to your clipboard. To get a saved file, you need one extra step after snipping.
1
Take your snip as usual (Win + Shift + S), then look for a small notification in the bottom-right corner of your screen.

As soon as you finish your snip, a small preview thumbnail pops up in the bottom-right corner. Left-click on it before it disappears — this opens your snip in the Snipping Tool app. If you missed it, search "Snipping Tool" in your Start menu to open it.

2
In the Snipping Tool window, left-click the Save button (or press Ctrl + S).

A "Save As" window will appear. Save the file to your Desktop — it's the easiest place to find it again. You can name it something you'll recognize, like "worksheet question," or just leave the default name.

3
Open Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or Copilot and left-click the 📎 paperclip icon inside the chat box.

Look at the bottom of the chat input box — you'll see a small paperclip icon (📎). Left-click it once. A file browser window will open.

4
In the file browser, left-click "Desktop" on the left, then double-click your saved snip.

Your screenshot file will appear in the files list. Double-click it — a thumbnail will appear in the chat box, showing the image is attached and ready. Now type your prompt and press Enter.

✅ On Mac, your snip is already saved automatically. Every time you take a snip with Cmd+Shift+4, Mac saves it as a file on your Desktop instantly — no extra steps needed.
1
Look on your Desktop for the file — it's named "Screenshot [date and time].png."

The most recent snip will always have today's date and the latest time in the name. If your Desktop is cluttered, you can also find it in Finder — it shows up near the top when sorted by Date Modified.

2

Step 5 of 7 — What to Screenshot

🎯 What to Screenshot and Why

Here are the most powerful ways teachers are using this technique:

📋
Question Format
"Make 10 more exactly like this one" — paste from any workbook, textbook, or worksheet.
📖
Reading Passage
"Write comprehension questions for this" — works on any article, story, or physical book page.
📝
Rubric
"Make a rubric like this for ___" — AI copies the structure and scoring scale exactly.
🗂️
Worksheet Layout
"Recreate this layout with new content" — columns, word banks, matching sections and all.
✏️
Student Work
"Give me feedback on this" — photo of a handwritten essay or math work (no names!).
📊
Test Question
"Explain why the answer is ___" — AI breaks down exactly what concept a student needs to understand.
📚
Story Pages
"Build a vocab list and comprehension questions" — any classroom reader or chapter book.
🌐
Website Style
"Build a site that looks like this" — screenshot a review game or site you love and AI copies the style.

💬 Real Prompts to Use After Uploading a Screenshot

  1. Take a snip of whatever you want AI to see — a question, a worksheet, a website, anything on your screen. (Use the shortcut you learned in the previous steps.)
  2. Open your AI tool in a new tab and left-click inside the chat box.
  3. Right-click in the chat box and choose Paste — your screenshot appears as an image in the chat.
  4. Pick a prompt below and left-click Copy.
  5. Right-click in the chat box again and choose Paste to add the prompt text below your image.
  6. Replace anything in [brackets] with your specifics, then hit Enter.
Copy a question format
I've attached a screenshot of a question I like. Please write 10 more questions about [TOPIC] for [GRADE] students using the exact same format — same question style, same number of answer choices, same labeling (A, B, C, D), and same overall structure.
Generate questions from a reading passage
I've attached a screenshot of a reading passage. Please: 1. Write 5 multiple choice comprehension questions based on this text 2. Write 2 short answer questions that require students to use evidence from the passage 3. Identify 5 vocabulary words from the passage that [GRADE] students might find challenging and write student-friendly definitions for each
Recreate a rubric structure
I've attached a screenshot of a rubric I like. Please create a new rubric for [ASSIGNMENT TYPE] for [GRADE] students using the exact same table structure, scoring scale, and category format shown in the image. Replace the categories with ones appropriate for [ASSIGNMENT TYPE].
Match a worksheet layout
I've attached a screenshot of a worksheet I like. Please create a new worksheet about [TOPIC] for [GRADE] that uses the same general layout — same column structure, same section organization, and same type of activities — but with completely new content about [TOPIC].
Feedback on student work
I've attached a photo of a student's [essay / math work / graphic organizer]. This student is in [GRADE]. Please: 1. Identify 2–3 specific strengths in their work 2. Identify 2–3 areas for growth 3. Suggest one next step I could teach this student Keep the feedback kind, specific, and actionable.
Explain a test question
I've attached a screenshot of a test question. The correct answer is [ANSWER]. Please explain in simple terms why that answer is correct, why each wrong answer is incorrect, and what concept a student needs to understand to get this type of question right. Write the explanation for a [GRADE] student.

Step 6 of 7 — Tips & Try It

💡 Tips for the Best Results

Snip closely around what matters

The more focused your screenshot, the better AI understands what to copy. If you want it to replicate a question format, crop the snip to just that one question — not the whole page.

Name what you're showing it

Start your prompt by telling AI what the image is: "This is a screenshot of a 4th grade reading comprehension question…" It helps AI pay attention to the right details.

You can upload multiple images

Want to show AI two examples so it can find the pattern? Upload both! You can say "Here are two examples of the question format I want. Make 8 more like these."

Photos of physical materials work too

Don't have a digital file? Take a photo with your phone of any printed worksheet, textbook page, or handwritten note. As long as the text is readable, AI can work with it.

⚠️ Privacy reminder: Before photographing or screenshotting student work, remove or cover any student names, ID numbers, or other identifying information. Never upload images that could identify a specific student.

⚡ The 4-Step "Show AI" Workflow

Here's the entire process from start to finish — it takes under 2 minutes once you know it:

1Find your example. Open the worksheet, website, book, or document that has the format or content you want to replicate.

2Snip it (also called a screenshot — same thing). Use the snipping tool to draw a box around just the section you want. The moment you let go, it copies to your clipboard — no file saved.

Windows: Win + Shift + S  ·  Mac: Cmd + Ctrl + Shift + 4

3Paste into AI. Open Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or Copilot, left-click in the chat box, right-click, and choose Paste.

4Type your prompt. Tell AI what to do with it — make more like this, generate questions from this, match this format, etc.

✅ That's it! Once you get comfortable with this workflow, it becomes second nature. Most experienced AI users say showing screenshots is the technique that saves them the most time.

Step 7 of 7 — Differentiating Assignments

🎯 Differentiating Assignments

Now that you know how to copy-paste text and share images with AI, you can use all three skills to take any assignment and create easier or harder versions — or adapt it for English learners. Choose the option that fits what you have:

✂️ Option 1 — Copy & Paste
Highlight and copy just the part you want AI to work on — a paragraph, a few questions, or one section.
Use when: you only care about the words — not how it looks. No columns, no formatting, no images. Just the text.
📸 Option 2 — Snip & Paste
Take a screenshot or snip of part of a digital file or website. Paste the image into AI.
Use when: you want AI to see a visual — a format, layout, or printed page
📎 Option 3 — Upload the File
Left-click the 📎 paperclip or ＋ plus icon in the AI chat box, find your Word doc, Google Doc, or PDF, and upload it. AI reads the whole thing.
Use when: you want AI to see the entire document at once

How to differentiate — step by step:

  1. Get your assignment ready. Open the digital file, paste your screenshot or snip, or have your file ready to upload.
  2. Share it with AI using the option that fits — copy-paste part of the text, take a snip/photo and paste it, or upload the whole file with the paperclip/plus icon.
  3. Pick a prompt below and left-click Copy.
  4. Right-click in your AI chat box and choose Paste.
  5. Replace [GRADE] with your grade level. If you used copy-paste (Option 1), also replace [PASTE YOUR ASSIGNMENT] by right-clicking and pasting your copied text. For Options 2 and 3, delete that placeholder — AI already has the content.
  6. Hit Enter and review the result. Ask AI to adjust anything that doesn't fit your class.
📋 Simplify for Struggling Readers  
Here is a [GRADE] assignment: [PASTE YOUR ASSIGNMENT]. Rewrite it at a lower reading level (approximately 2 grades below). Keep all the same content and questions, but use shorter sentences, simpler vocabulary, and add visual cues like bold key words.
📋 Extend for Advanced Students  
Here is a [GRADE] assignment: [PASTE YOUR ASSIGNMENT]. Create an extended version for advanced students. Add 2–3 higher-order thinking questions that require analysis, evaluation, or creative application of the concepts.
📋 Adapt for English Learners  
Here is a [GRADE] assignment: [PASTE YOUR ASSIGNMENT]. Adapt it for English Language Learners. Simplify sentence structures, define key vocabulary words in parentheses, and add sentence frames or word banks where helpful.
⚠️ Always review before using. Check the result for accuracy and age-appropriateness. AI gives you a strong first draft — you make the final call before handing anything to students.
