📸 Show AI Exactly What You Want

Use screenshots, snips, and photos to show AI a specific format, question style, or reading passage — so it copies your exact vision.

Step 1 of 5 — The Secret Superpower

🚀 Before you begin: Open one of these in a new tab — both are free. You'll use it to practice taking screenshots and uploading them as you go.
Claude (claude.ai) or ChatGPT (chatgpt.com)

🤯 The Secret Superpower: Showing Instead of Telling

You already know how to describe what you want in a prompt. But there's an even faster, more accurate method: show the AI a picture of it.

Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot, and Gemini can all read and understand images — including screenshots, photos of worksheets, and even pictures of handwritten notes.

📌 Real example from your classroom: Instead of spending three sentences trying to describe a specific question format, you snap a quick screenshot of one example question and say "Make me 10 more questions exactly like this one." Done in 10 seconds.

👁️ What Can AI Actually See in a Screenshot?

When you upload or paste an image into Claude, ChatGPT, or Copilot, the AI reads every detail — layout, text, formatting, structure, and style. Here's what it can pick up on:

  • The exact format of questions (multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank, matching, short answer, etc.)
  • The reading level and vocabulary of a passage
  • The design and layout of a worksheet (column structure, font size, spacing)
  • The number of answer choices and how they're labeled (A, B, C, D vs. 1, 2, 3, 4)
  • Whether questions use bold keywords, underlining, or specific phrasing styles
  • Rubric structures, table formats, graphic organizer layouts
✅ Think of it this way: A screenshot is worth a thousand words of prompting. If you have an example of what you want, showing it is always faster and more accurate than describing it.

Step 3 of 5 — Snip & Save

💾 Snip & Save — When You Want to Keep the File

📌 Paste vs. Save — which should you use?
  • Just paste it — when you need it once, right now. Quick question, quick answer. No file needed.
  • Save the file — when you want to use the same image more than once, share it with a colleague, or upload it to AI on a different day.

This step shows you how to find your saved snip and upload it to AI using the paperclip button. Select your computer type:

⚠️ On Windows, Win+Shift+S does NOT save a file automatically — it only copies to your clipboard. To get a saved file, you need one extra step after snipping.
1
Take your snip as usual (Win + Shift + S), then look for a small notification in the bottom-right corner of your screen.

As soon as you finish your snip, a small preview thumbnail pops up in the bottom-right corner. Click on it before it disappears — this opens your snip in the Snipping Tool app. If you missed it, search "Snipping Tool" in your Start menu to open it.

2
In the Snipping Tool window, click the Save button (or press Ctrl + S).

A "Save As" window will appear. Save the file to your Desktop — it's the easiest place to find it again. You can name it something you'll recognize, like "worksheet question," or just leave the default name.

3
Open Claude, ChatGPT, or Copilot and click the 📎 paperclip icon inside the chat box.

Look at the bottom of the chat input box — you'll see a small paperclip icon (📎). Click it once. A file browser window will open.

4
In the file browser, click "Desktop" on the left, then double-click your saved snip.

Your screenshot file will appear in the files list. Double-click it — a thumbnail will appear in the chat box, showing the image is attached and ready. Now type your prompt and press Enter.

Step 4 of 5 — What to Screenshot

🎯 What to Screenshot and Why

Here are the most powerful ways teachers are using this technique:

📋

Screenshot a question format → "Make more like this"

Found a question style you love in a workbook, textbook, or online? Snip it and ask AI to write 10 more in the exact same format.

📖

Screenshot a reading passage → "Write comprehension questions for this"

Snap a photo of any reading story or article — even from a physical book — and ask AI to generate questions, summaries, or vocabulary lists based on that exact text.

📚

Screenshot pages of a story → "Build a vocabulary list and comprehension questions"

Take a photo of one or more pages from any story — a classroom reader, a chapter book, even a picture book — and ask AI to pull out challenging vocabulary words with definitions, then write comprehension questions at exactly the right level for your students. You can do this for any story you already use, without ever having to retype the text.

📝

Screenshot a rubric → "Make a rubric like this for ___"

Show AI a rubric you already like and ask it to create a new one with the same structure for a different assignment or subject.

🗂️

Screenshot a worksheet layout → "Recreate this layout with new content"

Show AI a worksheet design — two columns, matching section, word bank at top — and ask it to recreate the same layout for a different topic.

✏️

Photo of student work → "Give me feedback on this"

Take a photo of a student's handwritten essay or math work (no names!) and ask AI to suggest feedback, identify misconceptions, or suggest the next teaching step.

📊

Screenshot a test question → "Explain why the answer is ___"

Snap a tricky standardized test question and ask AI to explain the correct answer and the reasoning behind it — perfect for test prep coaching.

🌐

Screenshot a website style → "Build a site that looks like this"

See a review game or classroom website you love? Screenshot it and ask AI to build something similar with your own content.

💬 Real Prompts to Use After Uploading a Screenshot

Upload your screenshot first, then click Copy on one of these prompts and paste it into the chat. Replace the [brackets] with your specifics before you hit send.

Copy a question format
I've attached a screenshot of a question I like. Please write 10 more questions about [TOPIC] for [GRADE] students using the exact same format — same question style, same number of answer choices, same labeling (A, B, C, D), and same overall structure.
Generate questions from a reading passage
I've attached a screenshot of a reading passage. Please: 1. Write 5 multiple choice comprehension questions based on this text 2. Write 2 short answer questions that require students to use evidence from the passage 3. Identify 5 vocabulary words from the passage that [GRADE] students might find challenging and write student-friendly definitions for each
Recreate a rubric structure
I've attached a screenshot of a rubric I like. Please create a new rubric for [ASSIGNMENT TYPE] for [GRADE] students using the exact same table structure, scoring scale, and category format shown in the image. Replace the categories with ones appropriate for [ASSIGNMENT TYPE].
Match a worksheet layout
I've attached a screenshot of a worksheet I like. Please create a new worksheet about [TOPIC] for [GRADE] that uses the same general layout — same column structure, same section organization, and same type of activities — but with completely new content about [TOPIC].
Feedback on student work
I've attached a photo of a student's [essay / math work / graphic organizer]. This student is in [GRADE]. Please: 1. Identify 2–3 specific strengths in their work 2. Identify 2–3 areas for growth 3. Suggest one next step I could teach this student Keep the feedback kind, specific, and actionable.
Explain a test question
I've attached a screenshot of a test question. The correct answer is [ANSWER]. Please explain in simple terms why that answer is correct, why each wrong answer is incorrect, and what concept a student needs to understand to get this type of question right. Write the explanation for a [GRADE] student.

Step 5 of 5 — Tips & Try It

💡 Tips for the Best Results

Snip closely around what matters

The more focused your screenshot, the better AI understands what to copy. If you want it to replicate a question format, crop the snip to just that one question — not the whole page.

Name what you're showing it

Start your prompt by telling AI what the image is: "This is a screenshot of a 4th grade reading comprehension question…" It helps AI pay attention to the right details.

You can upload multiple images

Want to show AI two examples so it can find the pattern? Upload both! You can say "Here are two examples of the question format I want. Make 8 more like these."

Photos of physical materials work too

Don't have a digital file? Take a photo with your phone of any printed worksheet, textbook page, or handwritten note. As long as the text is readable, AI can work with it.

⚠️ Privacy reminder: Before photographing or screenshotting student work, remove or cover any student names, ID numbers, or other identifying information. Never upload images that could identify a specific student.

⚡ The 4-Step "Show AI" Workflow

Here's the entire process from start to finish — it takes under 2 minutes once you know it:

1Find your example. Open the worksheet, website, book, or document that has the format or content you want to replicate.

2Snip it (also called a screenshot — same thing). Use the snipping tool to draw a box around just the section you want. The moment you let go, it copies to your clipboard — no file saved.

Windows: Win + Shift + S  ·  Mac: Cmd + Ctrl + Shift + 4

3Paste into AI. Open Claude, ChatGPT, or Copilot, click in the chat box, right-click, and choose Paste.

4Type your prompt. Tell AI what to do with it — make more like this, generate questions from this, match this format, etc.

✅ That's it! Once you get comfortable with this workflow, it becomes second nature. Most experienced AI users say showing screenshots is the technique that saves them the most time.

✏️ Try It Right Now

🧪 Your First Screenshot Challenge

Here's a quick practice activity to try before your next lesson:

  1. Find a question from any worksheet, textbook, or website that you like the format of.
  2. Take a snip of just that one question.
  3. Open Claude, ChatGPT, or Copilot and paste the snip.
  4. Type: "Write 5 more questions about [your topic] in the exact same format as this one."
  5. Review the result and refine if needed!
