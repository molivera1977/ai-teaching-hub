Step 1 of 6 — What is AI?

👋 Welcome! What IS AI?

You've probably seen and heard the term AI everywhere lately — on the news, on TV, maybe even from fellow teachers at lunch. But what does the term or letters even mean?

Artificial Intelligence

A computer built by humans specifically programmed to speak and act like a human — and it's here to be the best advisor you've ever had. It's programmed to not only be smarter than every human being, it's supposed to be as smart as every human being.... combined.

"AI in the teaching world is like having the smartest teaching assistant in the world — available any time of day, willing to help with every part of your job, and never complaining or running out of energy."

With something as simple as writing a text, a teacher can use AI to...

📅 Write me a lesson plan 📚 Build a 2-week unit 📝 Create a quiz 🎯 Make a rubric 💡 Give me 10 lesson ideas ✉️ Write a parent letter ✏️ Write a sub plan 📖 Summarize this article for my students 🗂️ Create a word search 🎓 Explain this concept in simpler terms

How simple is it to use?

⌨️
1. Type
Ask for what you need in plain English
⚡
2. Wait 5 seconds
AI thinks and writes a response
✅
3. Use it
Edit, copy, or ask for changes

Ready to try it right now? Here's all you do:

  1. Open a new browser tab and go to one of these free tools — chatgpt.com, claude.ai, gemini.google.com, or copilot.microsoft.com.
  2. Create a free account using your personal email. It takes about two minutes.
  3. Left-click inside the chat box at the bottom of the screen.
  4. Type anything you'd ask a knowledgeable colleague — in plain English, no special wording needed.
  5. Hit Enter and wait a few seconds while AI writes a response.
  6. Read the response. If you want something different, just type what you'd like changed and hit Enter again.

Step 2 of 6 — Why Teachers Love AI

🌟 Why do teachers love using AI?

Here's the honest truth: teachers don't need AI to be great at their job. But once they try it, most never want to go back. Here's why.

📅

Full lesson plans in minutes

Tell AI your topic, your grade level, and how long the lesson should be — and it writes you a complete lesson plan with an objective, activities, and a closing. What used to take an hour takes 5 minutes.

📚

Entire units of study, built to order

This is the one that blows teachers' minds. Tell AI: "Build me a 2-week unit on the American Revolution for 4th grade" — and it maps out every day, every activity, every objective. You can even say how many days per week, what subjects to cover, and what standards to hit.

🎯

Different versions for different students

Need the same reading passage at three different reading levels? AI does all three at once. Every student gets something that fits them — without you rewriting everything by hand.

📝

Quizzes, rubrics, and worksheets on demand

Type what you need and AI builds it. A 10-question quiz on fractions, a rubric for a science project, a writing prompt for Monday morning — ready in seconds.

💡

A brainstorming partner that never gets tired

Stuck on how to introduce a tricky concept? Ask AI for 10 ideas. Hate them all? Ask for 10 more. It never runs out of suggestions and never minds being told to try again.

🔧

You're always the one in charge

AI gives you a starting point — you decide what stays, what changes, and what gets tossed. Nothing goes to students without your eyes on it first.

🧠

It learns you — and remembers what you like

As you work with AI, you can tell it exactly how you like things done. Prefer your quizzes in a certain format? Want your reading passages to always have a vocabulary section at the bottom? Just tell it — and it will remember. The more you share your preferences, the more it feels like working with someone who already knows your style.

…and these are just a handful of the hundreds of reasons teachers fall in love with AI once they give it a try.

⚠️ One important thing to know right away

AI can get things wrong. It can make up facts, misspell names, or give you something that just doesn't quite fit.

👀

Always read what it gives you before sharing it with students — think of it like proofreading a lesson plan from a substitute.

💡

Here's a trick: you can ask AI to double-check the very thing it just gave you. It won't get offended. Try:

"Read that back and check it for any factual errors or awkward phrasing."

Step 5 of 6 — Your First Step

🚀 Your First Step

4 simple steps. Takes about 2 minutes. No downloads. No credit card.

1
🌐 Open your web browser
You're already doing this step — it's the app you're using right now to read this page. Chrome, Safari, and Edge are the most common ones.
2
🔗 Go to the website
Left-click the long white bar at the very top of your screen, type one of these addresses, and press Enter — that bar is called the address bar:
claude.ai chatgpt.com gemini.google.com copilot.microsoft.com
3
📝 Create a free account
Left-click Sign Up or Get Started and enter your email. Many sites also offer shortcuts so you don't have to create a new password at all:
🔵 "Sign in with Google" — uses your existing Google account. No new password needed.
🍎 "Sign in with Apple" — uses your existing Apple ID. Works great on iPhones and Macs.
Any of these options work perfectly — use whichever feels easiest.
4
💬 Say hello
Left-click inside the typing box at the bottom of the screen, type anything — even just Hello — and press Enter. That's it. You're using AI. 🎉

🔓 When Free Starts Feeling Limiting

Free plans are a great way to get started — and for many tasks, they're honestly enough. But once you're using AI regularly, you'll start bumping into the same walls. Here's what to expect, and what changes when you upgrade.

ChatGPT Free for K-12 Teachers

Free limits: Slower responses, usage caps during busy hours, no image generation.

With the teacher plan (free through 2027): Full GPT-4o, image creation, web browsing, downloadable Word & PDF files, and no usage caps. Verify with your school email at chatgpt.com/edu.

Claude ~$20/mo

Free limits: You'll hit daily message limits faster than you expect, especially for long conversations. No downloadable Word or PDF files.

Pro plan adds: Much higher usage limits, downloadable files, Projects (so Claude remembers your class setup between sessions), and access to the most powerful Claude model.

Google Gemini Often free at school

Free limits: Basic model only, no Deep Research, no Gemini inside Google Docs or Gmail.

Google One AI Premium ($20/mo) or Google Workspace for Education adds: Gemini built into every Google app you already use — write in Docs, summarize in Gmail, create slides from a prompt. Ask your tech person if your school already has it.

Microsoft Copilot Often free at school

Free limits: Works as a standalone chat tool. The magic — Copilot inside Word, Teams, Outlook — requires a Microsoft 365 plan.

Microsoft 365 Copilot adds: AI directly inside Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Teams, and Outlook. Draft a lesson plan in Word, summarize a parent email in Outlook, or turn bullet points into a full presentation. Many schools already pay for this — worth asking your tech person.

Canva Free for teachers

Free limits: Paid templates and premium elements are locked, no AI image generation, limited storage.

Canva for Education (free with school verification): Unlocks the full Pro version — AI image generation, background remover, thousands of premium templates, and the ability to assign Canva projects directly to students. Completely free for verified teachers.

Perplexity $10/mo w/ school email

Free limits: Limited searches per day, no Deep Research, no image generation, basic AI models only.

Pro plan ($10/mo with school email): Unlimited searches, Deep Research for thorough multi-source reports, image generation, and access to Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini all from one place — useful if you want to compare answers without juggling accounts.

💡 The honest take: Start free. Use it enough to know which tool fits your style. Then upgrade the one you actually reach for — not all six.
