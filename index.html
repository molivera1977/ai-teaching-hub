Step 1 of 4 — What is AI?

👋 Welcome! What even IS AI?

You've probably seen the word AI everywhere lately — on the news, in your email, maybe even in the teachers' lounge. But what is it, really?

Here's the best way we've heard it described: AI is like having the smartest teaching assistant in the world — one who is willing to help you with every single aspect of your job, any time of day, without ever complaining or running out of energy.

Need a lesson plan? Ask. Need a quiz? Ask. Need 10 different ideas for introducing fractions, a rubric for a writing assignment, or a two-week unit on habitats? Just ask — and you'll have it in seconds.

You talk to it in plain English, just like sending a text. There's no special language to learn, no buttons to figure out. If you can type, you can use AI.

🙋 Feeling a little nervous? That's totally normal. Almost every teacher feels that way at first. But here's the truth: you don't need to know anything about technology, computers, or coding. This course will walk you through everything step by step — and you'll be amazed how quickly it clicks.
💬 Remember what we tell the kids — there are no stupid questions. If you don't know something, just ask AI. It doesn't judge, it doesn't get impatient, and it will give you the best answer it possibly can. Not sure what a word means? Ask. Don't know how to start a lesson on a topic you've never taught? Ask. Wondering if something you wrote sounds good? Ask. That's exactly what it's there for.

And here's the best part — even if you don't know what to ask, AI can help you figure that out too. Just say something like "I need to teach a unit on weather but I'm not sure where to start — what questions should I be thinking about?" and it will brainstorm the right questions with you. You're never on your own.

Step 2 of 4 — Why Teachers Love AI

🌟 Why do teachers love using AI?

Here's the honest truth: teachers don't need AI to be great at their job. But once they try it, most never want to go back. Here's why.

📅

Full lesson plans in minutes

Tell AI your topic, your grade level, and how long the lesson should be — and it writes you a complete lesson plan with an objective, activities, and a closing. What used to take an hour takes 5 minutes.

📚

Entire units of study, built to order

This is the one that blows teachers' minds. Tell AI: "Build me a 2-week unit on the American Revolution for 4th grade" — and it maps out every day, every activity, every objective. You can even say how many days per week, what subjects to cover, and what standards to hit.

🎯

Different versions for different students

Need the same reading passage at three different reading levels? AI does all three at once. Every student gets something that fits them — without you rewriting everything by hand.

📝

Quizzes, rubrics, and worksheets on demand

Type what you need and AI builds it. A 10-question quiz on fractions, a rubric for a science project, a writing prompt for Monday morning — ready in seconds.

💡

A brainstorming partner that never gets tired

Stuck on how to introduce a tricky concept? Ask AI for 10 ideas. Hate them all? Ask for 10 more. It never runs out of suggestions and never minds being told to try again.

🔧

You're always the one in charge

AI gives you a starting point — you decide what stays, what changes, and what gets tossed. Nothing goes to students without your eyes on it first.

🧠

It learns you — and remembers what you like

As you work with AI, you can tell it exactly how you like things done. Prefer your quizzes in a certain format? Want your reading passages to always have a vocabulary section at the bottom? Just tell it — and it will remember. The more you share your preferences, the more it feels like working with someone who already knows your style.

…and these are just a handful of the hundreds of reasons teachers fall in love with AI once they give it a try.

⚠️ One important thing to know right away AI can get things wrong. It can make up facts, misspell names, or give you something that just doesn't quite fit. Always read what it gives you before sharing it with students — think of it like proofreading a lesson plan from a substitute.

Your First Step

🚀 Ready to get started? It takes 2 minutes.

You don't need to download anything. You don't need a credit card. Just open your browser and follow these steps:

  1. 1 Open any web browser — Chrome, Safari, Edge, whatever you normally use.
  2. 2 Go to claude.ai, chatgpt.com, gemini.google.com, copilot.microsoft.com, or perplexity.ai and click the Sign Up button.
  3. 3 Enter your email and create a password — or click "Sign in with Google," which works for all four services.
  4. 4 A chat box appears. Type anything and press Enter. You're using AI.
💡 Our recommendation: start with Claude. It's the most beginner-friendly tool, it works great for every lesson in this course, and the free version gives you plenty to get started — no credit card needed.
